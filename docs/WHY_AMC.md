# WHY_AMC.md — Why AMC exists

AMC exists because AI agents are too often judged by what they claim instead of what they actually do.

That is the trust gap.

## The short version
Most agent evaluation stories today are inflated by:
- self-reported documentation
- checklists filled in by the team being judged
- keyword scans that confuse policy text with real controls
- demos that look safe without proving operational behavior

AMC is built to close that gap.

## What AMC does differently
AMC centers trust on **execution evidence**.

That means:
- score from observed behavior, not vibes
- weigh evidence by trustworthiness
- keep proof paths and integrity in view
- surface the gaps that actually matter next

## What you get
AMC helps you:
1. **Score** an agent from evidence
2. **Find** what is missing, weak, or risky
3. **Generate** fixes, guardrails, reports, and next steps
4. **Expand** into CI, assurance, governance, observability, and compliance when needed

## Why this matters
A system that says “trust me” is not a trust system.
A system that shows evidence, provenance, and gaps is.

That is the whole AMC thesis:

> Score AI agents from evidence. Find the gaps. Fix what matters.
