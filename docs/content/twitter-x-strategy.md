# Twitter/X Strategy for AMC (AMC-32)

## Account Setup (Needs Sid)
- Handle: @AMCscore or @AgentMaturityCompass or @AgentMaturity
- Bio: "The credit score for AI agents. Evidence over claims. Open source, MIT licensed. Built by @[Sid's handle]"
- Banner: AMC terminal screenshot or logo
- Pinned tweet: Launch announcement thread

## Content Strategy

### Voice & Tone
- Technical but accessible
- Direct, no fluff ("Here's what we built and why")
- Show don't tell (screenshots, terminal output, code snippets)
- Engage with AI safety / agent builder discourse

### Content Pillars (recurring themes)
1. **The Evidence Thesis** — why self-reported safety is broken
2. **Build in public** — share development progress, test results, user feedback
3. **Agent safety education** — explain threats (prompt injection, exfiltration, alignment faking)
4. **Comparison & integration** — how AMC works with other tools
5. **Compliance updates** — EU AI Act timeline, regulatory news

### Post Frequency
- Launch week: 2-3 tweets/day
- Ongoing: 1 tweet/day minimum
- Threads: 2/week

### Engagement Strategy
- Reply to every mention within 2 hours
- Quote-tweet interesting AI safety discussions with AMC perspective
- Follow and engage with: AI safety researchers, agent framework maintainers, compliance professionals
- Don't engage with trolls or bad-faith criticism
