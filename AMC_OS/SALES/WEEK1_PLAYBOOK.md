## SPIN-BASED CALL SCRIPTS

### Script 1: Cold Discovery (25 min) — ICP 1: AI-First Startup

**Opening (2 min):**
"Hi [Name], thanks for the time. I'll keep this practical — my goal is to understand where your agent program has the most exposure right now, and whether a 5-day diagnostic sprint makes sense. I'll ask a few questions, mirror back what I hear, and we'll decide together. Sound good?"

**Situation (3 min):**
- "What AI agent workflows are in production today, and what do they touch?"
- "Who owns the decision when an agent takes a high-risk action — payment, external API call, customer-facing output?"
- "Do you currently track agent reliability, cost, or quality with any structured scoring?"

**Problem (5 min):**
- "Where have things broken recently? Silent failures, hallucinations, cost spikes?"
- "When an incident happens, what's the mean time to identify root cause?"
- "What's not measured today that you wish you had visibility on tomorrow?"

**Implication (5 min):**
- "If these gaps persist for another 60 days, what gets worse — cost, trust, team time, customer risk?"
- "How does leadership react when you say the system is 'stable' but can't back it with evidence?"
- "What's the risk if a board member or investor asks 'what's the maturity of our AI program' and you don't have a data-backed answer?"

**Need-Payoff (3 min):**
- "If we could give you an evidence-backed maturity score across 7 dimensions in 5 business days, what decision would that unlock?"
- "Would a prioritized 30/60/90 roadmap with owners reduce your planning uncertainty this quarter?"
- "If the top 3 gaps were ranked by business impact with clear next steps, how would that change your team's focus?"

**Close (2 min):**
"Based on what you shared, [summarize 2–3 pains in their words]. The fastest way to get clarity is the AMC Compass Sprint — 5 days, $5,000 fixed, 2 hours of your team's time. I can send a proposal today and we'd kick off [date]. Does that align with what you need?"

## OBJECTION HANDLING — TOP 5

### Objection 1: "We don't need this"

**What they mean:** "I don't see the problem clearly enough to spend money on it."

**Response:**
> "That's fair — and honestly, the teams that need this most often feel that way because the gaps aren't visible until something breaks. Let me ask: if your CEO asked you today 'rate our AI agent maturity 1–5 with evidence' — could you answer confidently? [Pause.] The Sprint doesn't create a problem — it reveals whether one exists. If we score you at L4–L5 across all dimensions, you've validated your program for $5k. If we find gaps, you've caught them before your customers or regulators do."

**Fallback:** "Would a 15-minute maturity gut-check call be worth it? No commitment — just tell me about your setup and I'll tell you where most teams at your stage have blind spots."

---

### Objection 2: "Too expensive"

**What they mean:** "I don't see enough value relative to the price, OR I can't easily approve this amount."

**Response:**
> "Understood — let me reframe the math. What does one week of a senior engineer's time cost you, fully loaded? [$3k–$5k.] The Sprint replaces 2–4 weeks of your team trying to figure out what to prioritize. If it prevents even one misdirected sprint, it's paid for itself. And if an agent fails in front of a customer next month, what's that cost — in engineering time, trust, and leadership confidence?"

**Concession ladder (in order, never skip ahead):**
1. Payment split: $2,500 now / $2,500 on delivery — total stays $5,000
2. Scope narrow: Focus on top 3 dimensions instead of 7 — price adjusts to $3,500
3. Path downgrade: 90-min AI Readiness Session for $1,500 — produces gap list + Sprint recommendation
4. Time-bound add-on: Approve by [date] → free second executive readout session

**Never:** Discount spontaneously. Always trade for scope, terms, or timeline.

---

### Objection 3: "We'll just use vendor scorecards"

**What they mean:** "Our existing tool vendors provide assessments — why pay for another one?"

**Response:**
> "Vendor scorecards are useful for what they cover — but they score you against their product's capabilities, not your program's maturity. That's like your car dealer grading your driving — they'll always recommend their parts. The AMC framework covers 7 dimensions including governance, business alignment, and cross-vendor observability. Vendor scorecards typically hit 1–2 of those. The real question is: do vendor scorecards tell you what to do next across your entire agent program, with owners and priorities? If yes, you're ahead of most teams. If not, the Sprint fills that gap."

**Proof move:** "I can show you the 7 dimensions side-by-side with what [Vendor X] covers. Usually teams find 4–5 dimensions with zero vendor coverage."

---

### Objection 4: "We'll build our own"

**What they mean:** "We have smart engineers and we'd rather invest internally."

**Response:**
> "That's a strong instinct — and it works when you have a proven rubric to build against. Here's the pattern I see: teams spend 4–8 weeks building an internal assessment framework, it covers the dimensions they already know about, and it misses the ones they don't. The Sprint gives your team a calibrated external baseline in 5 days — your engineers then build on top of that, not from scratch. Think of it as buying the map before you start the hike."

**Tailored close:** "Would it be useful to run the Sprint first, then hand the framework to your team to own and extend? That's how most teams use it — Sprint is the seed, your internal team is the garden."

---

### Objection 5: "Not a priority now"

**What they mean:** "We have bigger fires / this isn't urgent enough to act on today."

**Response:**
> "Totally respect that — I don't want to create false urgency. But let me ask: what's on fire right now? [Listen.] In my experience, the reason maturity assessment feels low-priority is because the consequences are invisible — until they're not. Silent agent failures, governance gaps, cost drift — they compound quietly. The Sprint takes 2 hours of your team's time across 5 days. It's designed to run alongside your current priorities, not compete with them."

**Timing pivot:** "If not this week, when does this become relevant? Is there a board meeting, audit, or launch date that creates a natural deadline? Let's set a checkpoint for [date] so this doesn't fall off the radar."

**Calendar close:** "Can I send a calendar hold for a 15-min check-in in [2–3 weeks]? If priorities shift, we'll be ready to move fast."
