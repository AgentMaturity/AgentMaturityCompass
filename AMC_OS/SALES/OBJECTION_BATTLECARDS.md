# Objection Battlecards — AMC Compass Sprint

*Methodology: Challenger Sale (Teach → Tailor → Take Control)*

---

## 1. "We already do this internally."

**What they're really saying:** "I don't see why I'd pay you when my team can assess themselves."

**Teach:**
> "That's actually great — it means you already care about maturity, which puts you ahead of 80% of teams I talk to. Here's the pattern I see though: internal assessments tend to anchor on what the team knows they built. The gaps that matter are in the dimensions nobody owns — governance, evaluation rigor, observability coverage. It's not a skill problem; it's the same reason you don't audit your own books."

**Tailor:**
> "What does your internal assessment cover today? I'd be curious whether it hits all 7 dimensions we score — most internal reviews cover 2-3 well and miss the rest entirely."

**Take Control:**
> "What if we ran the Sprint alongside your internal assessment? You'd get a calibrated external benchmark in 5 days. If our findings match yours, you've validated your process for $5k. If they don't, you've found blind spots before they become incidents."

---

## 2. "We can't afford $5k right now."

**What they're really saying:** "I don't see enough value to prioritize this spend."

**Teach:**
> "Totally fair. Let me reframe the math: what does one week of a senior engineer's time cost you — fully loaded? [$3k-$5k typically.] The Sprint replaces 2-4 weeks of your team trying to figure out what to focus on next. If it saves even one sprint of misdirected effort, it's paid for itself."

**SPIN Implication:**
> "What happens if you don't assess maturity and an agent fails in front of a customer next month? What's the cost of that — in engineering time, customer trust, and leadership confidence?"

**Take Control:**
> "I can do 50/50 payment — $2,500 now, $2,500 on delivery. That way you're only fully committed once you see the deliverables. Does that make it workable?"

---

## 3. "Why should I trust you?"

**What they're really saying:** "I don't know you. Prove you're credible."

**Teach:**
> "You shouldn't trust me based on a call — you should trust the framework and the deliverables. The AMC scoring model is evidence-backed: every L1-L5 score is tied to something observable in your environment, not my opinion. If the evidence doesn't support a score, we don't assign it."

**Proof points:**
> "[Reference specific experience: frameworks built, teams assessed, industries worked in.] But more importantly — here's our guarantee: we deliver every deliverable listed in the SOW or we keep working until we do. No time limit. If the work isn't good, you don't pay the second half."

**Take Control:**
> "What would help you get comfortable? I can share a sample deliverable — a redacted scorecard from a prior engagement — so you can see the quality before committing."

---

## 4. "How is this different from a consulting report?"

**What they're really saying:** "I've been burned by expensive PDFs that collect dust."

**Teach:**
> "I've seen those reports too — 60 pages of 'you should consider improving your observability.' Here's how the Sprint is different: every deliverable is specific and actionable. The scorecard isn't 'you need better monitoring' — it's 'you're at L2 on observability because you have logging but no alerting on agent failure rates, and here's the evidence.' The roadmap isn't 'improve governance' — it's 'by Day 30, implement human-in-the-loop approval for agent X; by Day 60, deploy cost-per-query tracking.'"

**Tailor:**
> "The exec deck is board-ready on Day 5 — not a draft you spend two weeks polishing. The technical appendix gives your engineers enough detail to start executing on Day 6."

**Take Control:**
> "If the deliverables aren't specific enough to act on, we haven't met our own acceptance criteria and we'll revise them. That's in the SOW."

---

## 5. "We need legal/procurement to review."

**What they're really saying:** This is either genuine process or a stall. Treat it as genuine first.

**Accelerate:**
> "Completely expected. I have a SOW template ready — clean, standard terms: IP protection, confidentiality, limitation of liability, 50/50 payment. Most legal teams turn it around in 2-3 days. Can I send it now so they can start while we're still aligned on timing?"

**Champion enablement:**
> "Would it help if I put together a one-page internal brief you can forward? Something that covers: what it is, what it costs, what you get, and why now? That usually speeds up internal approval."

**Take Control:**
> "While legal reviews, let's lock in a tentative start date. If the SOW is signed by [DATE], we can kick off [NEXT MONDAY]. Fair?"

---

## 6. "What's the ROI?"

**What they're really saying:** "Justify this spend to me (or to someone above me)."

**Honest answer:**
> "I'm going to give you the honest version, not the consulting version. I can't guarantee a specific ROI number because the value depends on what you do with the findings. What I can tell you is what teams typically get:"

**Value articulation:**
> "1. **Avoided misdirection** — teams report saving 2-4 weeks of engineering effort by knowing what to prioritize. At your team size, that's [$X-$Y].
> 2. **Incident prevention** — the gap analysis catches governance and observability gaps before they become customer-facing. One avoided incident is worth [their estimate from discovery call].
> 3. **Board/leadership confidence** — you walk out of the Sprint with a deck that answers 'how mature is our AI program?' That's not ROI you can put a dollar on, but it's credibility you can't buy.
> 4. **Speed** — instead of spending a month figuring out where you stand, you know in 5 days."

**Take Control:**
> "The real question isn't 'what's the ROI of an assessment?' — it's 'what's the cost of not knowing where you stand?' You told me earlier [reference their pain]. How much longer do you want to operate without that clarity?"

---

## 7. "We're too early stage."

**What they're really saying:** "We don't think we're complex enough to need this."

**Reframe:**
> "Actually, early stage is the ideal time. Here's why: right now you're making foundational decisions about agent architecture, monitoring, and governance. Those decisions compound. Assessing at L1-L2 means you build the right scaffolding from Day 1 instead of retrofitting at L3-L4 when it's 10x harder."

**Teach:**
> "The teams that struggle most are the ones who waited until they had 10 agents in production to think about maturity. By then, tech debt is structural and governance is bolted on, not built in."

**Take Control:**
> "The Sprint at your stage is faster and cheaper to act on — your roadmap items are foundational, not remedial. And you'll have a maturity story for your next funding round. When are you raising next?"

---

## 8. "We use [Competitor X]."

**What they're really saying:** "We already have a solution / Why switch?"

**Honest differentiation:**
> "What does [Competitor X] cover for you? I want to make sure I'm not overlapping with something that's already working."

*Listen. Then:*

> "Most tools I see in this space do one of two things well: monitoring/observability OR evaluation/testing. The AMC framework covers 7 dimensions because maturity isn't just technical — it's governance, business alignment, and operational rigor too. [Competitor X] is great at [what they actually do]; the Sprint complements it by giving you the full picture and showing you where [Competitor X] fits in your maturity journey."

**Take Control:**
> "Think of it this way: [Competitor X] is a tool. The Sprint is a diagnostic that tells you whether you're using that tool (and every other tool) effectively. They're complementary, not competing."

---

## Quick Reference: Objection → Key Move

| Objection | Primary Move | Emotional Core |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| "We do this internally" | External calibration = blind spot detection | Fear of unknown unknowns |
| "Can't afford $5k" | Cost of inaction > cost of Sprint | Budget anxiety |
| "Why trust you?" | Evidence-backed framework + guarantee | Risk aversion |
| "Just a consulting report" | Specificity of deliverables | Past disappointment |
| "Need legal/procurement" | Accelerate + arm champion | Process friction |
| "What's the ROI?" | Honest value articulation | Justification pressure |
| "Too early stage" | Early = cheaper to fix + investor story | Timing uncertainty |
| "Use Competitor X" | Complementary, not competing | Switching cost fear |
