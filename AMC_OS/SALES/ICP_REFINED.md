# Ideal Customer Profiles — AMC Compass Sprint

## ICP 1: AI-First Startups

**Profile:** Series A–B startups, 20–200 engineers, AI agents in production or launching within 60 days.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company size** | 20–200 engineers, $5M–$50M raised |
| **Stage** | Agents live or shipping within 60 days |
| **Pain** | Silent failures in production nobody catches until a customer complains. No governance framework — "we'll figure it out later." Investor/board pressure for reliability and cost control as they scale. |
| **Trigger signals** | New agent launch announced (blog, Product Hunt, changelog). Post-incident (downtime, hallucination incident, cost spike). Board meeting coming up. New VP Eng or CTO hire. |
| **Decision maker** | CTO or VP Engineering |
| **Champion** | Staff Engineer, Head of Platform, or ML Lead |
| **Budget authority** | $5k is a "just do it" expense at this stage — no procurement needed |
| **ACV** | $5k Sprint + $3k/mo retainer = $41k first-year potential |
| **Objection to expect** | "We're moving too fast to stop and assess" → Reframe: Sprint runs parallel, 2 hours of your time |
| **Where to find them** | Y Combinator portfolio, a]16z AI companies, Product Hunt AI launches, AI Engineer conference attendees, r/MachineLearning, Hacker News "Show HN" |

### Job-to-be-Done (JTBD)
*"When I'm scaling my agent from demo to production, I want to know what's going to break before it breaks, so I can ship with confidence and keep my board off my back."*

## ICP 2: Enterprise AI Teams

**Profile:** Fortune 500 or large enterprise with a dedicated AI/ML team (10–100+ ML engineers), deploying agents across business units.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company size** | 1,000+ employees, dedicated AI/ML org |
| **Stage** | Multiple agents in production or pilots across BUs |
| **Pain** | Compliance and audit pressure (SOC2, ISO, industry-specific). Cost overruns with no clear attribution per agent. Evaluation gaps — no way to compare agent quality across teams. Governance theater — policies exist on paper, not in practice. |
| **Trigger signals** | Failed internal or external audit. Agent cost spike ($50k+ unexpected). Agent producing wrong outputs caught by customer/regulator. New Chief AI Officer or Head of AI hire. AI governance mandate from board/legal. |
| **Decision maker** | Head of AI, Chief AI Officer, VP of ML/AI Platform |
| **Champion** | AI Platform Lead, ML Ops Engineer, AI Governance Lead |
| **Budget authority** | $10k+ requires procurement but is routine for consulting |
| **ACV** | $10k Sprint + $5k–$10k/mo retainer = $70k–$130k first-year potential |
| **Objection to expect** | "We need procurement/legal review" → Expected; provide SOW template, security questionnaire answers, champion enablement |
| **Where to find them** | LinkedIn (title search: "Head of AI," "Chief AI Officer"), AI/ML conferences (NeurIPS, ICML corporate track), Gartner/Forrester AI reports, enterprise AI Slack communities |

### Job-to-be-Done (JTBD)
*"When my AI team is deploying agents across business units, I want a standardized way to measure and compare maturity, so I can report progress to the board and catch problems before regulators do."*

## ICP 3: AI Consultancies & Agencies

**Profile:** Consulting firms and agencies that build/deploy AI for clients and want a standardized assessment framework.

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company size** | 10–500 consultants/engineers |
| **Stage** | Delivering AI agent projects to multiple clients |
| **Pain** | No standardized way to assess client readiness before engagement. Competitive proposals all look the same — no differentiator. Client expectations misaligned with agent maturity reality. |
| **Trigger signals** | New client RFP or engagement kickoff. Lost deal where competitor had a structured assessment. Client agent failure mid-engagement. Growing AI practice seeking methodology. |
| **Decision maker** | Principal, Managing Director, Practice Lead |
| **Champion** | Senior Consultant, Engagement Manager |
| **Budget authority** | High — they bill this through to clients at markup |
| **ACV** | Revenue share or bulk license ($2k–$5k per client assessment, 10+ per year) |
| **Objection to expect** | "We want to white-label this" → That's the point; discuss licensing |
| **Where to find them** | LinkedIn (consulting firm + AI practice), Clutch.co AI agencies, partnership directories, conference sponsor lists |

## Disqualification Criteria (Do NOT Pursue)

- No agents in production or planned within 90 days (too early)
- Budget holder cannot be identified or accessed
- "Just exploring" with no timeline or pain
- Wants free advice disguised as "partnership discussion"
- Company has fewer than 10 engineers (unlikely to have agent complexity worth $5k)

## Prioritization for First $5k

**Fastest close path → ICP 1 (AI-first startups)**
- Shortest sales cycle (1–5 days)
- CTO can approve without procurement
- Pain is visceral and current
- $5k is a rounding error on their burn rate

**Highest LTV path → ICP 2 (Enterprise)**
- Longer sales cycle (2–6 weeks) but much larger retainer
- Start with ICP 1 to get cash flow, layer in ICP 2 pipeline

**Scalable path → ICP 3 (Agencies)**
- Medium sales cycle, but each deal multiplies reach
- Pursue after proving Sprint with 2–3 direct clients
