## Community 3: LinkedIn AI Communities

### Thought Leadership Strategy

**Post 1 (Day 1):**
> **Most agent programs fail silently.**
>
> Not because the models are bad — because maturity isn't measured.
>
> Teams ship agents. They work (mostly). Then 60 days later: reliability drifts, governance is ad-hoc, costs spiral, and nobody has an evidence trail.
>
> We studied 50+ HN threads and community discussions on agent deployment. The pattern is consistent:
> - No standardized way to measure agent maturity
> - Agents fail silently with no quality degradation alerts
> - Governance frameworks don't cover autonomous agents
> - Teams can't justify agent ROI to leadership
>
> The fix isn't more monitoring tools. It's a maturity model — the same thing that transformed DevOps (DORA metrics) and cloud (AWS Well-Architected).
>
> We're building that model. More on what we've learned in the comments.

**Post 2 (Day 3):**
> **"How do you know your agents are production-ready?"**
>
> I asked this to 10 AI leaders last week. The most common answer: "We don't, really."
>
> The 7 dimensions that separate production-ready from production-risky:
> 1. Reliability (uptime is table stakes — what about output quality consistency?)
> 2. Eval coverage (do you catch regressions before users do?)
> 3. Governance (can you answer an auditor's questions with evidence?)
> 4. Cost efficiency (do you know which agents are burning money?)
> 5. UX quality (does the agent actually help, or just respond?)
> 6. Operational excellence (incident response, ownership, debug tooling)
> 7. Trust & transparency (can stakeholders see how decisions are made?)
>
> Score yourself 1-5 on each. If you're below 3 on any dimension, that's your next priority.
