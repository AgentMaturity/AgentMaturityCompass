01 — Manifesto

Trust is not
a feature.
It's evidence.

The Problem

Every AI agent claims to be trustworthy. None can prove it. Self-reported safety is like a restaurant grading its own hygiene — the incentives are wrong and the evidence is missing.

The Principle

A credit score doesn't ask if you're good with money — it looks at evidence. AMC does the same for AI agents. Signed, hash-chained, tamper-evident evidence that anyone can verify independently.

The Difference

Other frameworks use convention. AMC uses cryptography. If someone changes a score, the Merkle chain breaks. No exceptions. No workarounds. Math, not promises.

02 — Anatomy of a Score

Three steps.
Zero trust required.

01

Diagnose

111 questions across 5 dimensions probe every aspect — error handling, data protection, decision tracing, cost efficiency, and continuous improvement. No hand-waving allowed.

02

Score

25 modules calculate maturity from Absent (0) to Optimizing (5). Each score maps to concrete evidence requirements — not subjective judgment, but verifiable criteria.

03

Prove

Every score ships with a Merkle-tree evidence chain. Ed25519 signed. Tamper-evident. Anyone can verify the assessment independently — no trust in the assessor required.
